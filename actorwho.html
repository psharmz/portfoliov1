Actor Who captures the serendipitous spirit of the Edinburgh Festivals by repurposing the iconic yet outdated red UK telephone booths as audition booths. By posting the auditions on Twitter for voting, Act Who allows anyone to participate in the Edinburgh Festivals, regardless of where they are. Actor Who recieved a Yellow Pencil by the D&AD for the BBC brief at the New Blood Awards.

Project Background

Actor Who is a transmedia story created for the BBC that captures the serendipitous spirit of the Edinburgh Festivals. By allowing both online and physical audiences to participate through viewing and voting on Twitter and auditioning in Edinburgh phone booths, the multi-platform experience lets participants work together to create a story. Actor Who allows anyone to live the story of the Edinburgh Festivals, regardless of where they are.

How It Works

Technical Details

Processing was used to create the phone booth auditioning experience for the prototype. We had three weeks to build the booth and complete the software development. Sensors were also programmed to detect when a person walks into the phone booth to trigger a phone ring and begin the experience. Checkout github for the code used in the prototype

User Testing

We tested our protoype phone booth experience at the Design with Data exhibition. Testing revealed some key insights for the next iteration of design and development. A key insight was auditioners wished they could view themselves while they recited the lines. They also wished they had someone to audition with rather than just saying the lines like a monologue. Many felt camera shy but were comfortable with recording voice.
