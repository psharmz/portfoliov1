Actor Who captures the serendipitous spirit of the Edinburgh Festivals by repurposing the iconic yet outdated red UK telephone booths as audition booths. By posting the auditions on Twitter for voting, Actor Who allows anyone to participate in the Edinburgh Festivals, regardless of where they are. Actor Who recieved a Yellow Pencil by the D&AD for the BBC brief at the New Blood Awards.

Design Opportunity and Proposal

Technical Details

Usability Testing

We tested our protoype phone booth experience at the Design with Data exhibition. Testing revealed some key insights for the next iteration of design and development that led to a product pivot as well as UX design improvements.

Starting with the Positives:

The enclosed space of a telephone booth felt intimate and warm

People loved the branding and concept

The experience embodied the serendipitous spirit of the Fringe

Room For Improvement

Many people were camera shy and asked if their voice could just be recorded

Some visitors did not find it obvious they needed to pick up the phone and tried just interacting with the screen

Many people wished they could replay their audition before submitting

Many people felt strange reading lines alone, unless it was a monologue.

ReCall

Based on the feedback we recieved, we decided shift our product design. Due to the hesitation of video recording, we asked how we could still provide a serendipitous, intimate, and fun phonebooth experience that was solely voice based. Because of the feedback that solo auditions felt awkward, we drifted away from an audition idea and shifted more to a product that allows visitors leave their memories in the phone booth.

ReCall Feedback

While Actor Who made users nervous, ReCall empowered users. If they didn't like a recording, they could re-record it. Improvements were also made to the directions on how to use the keypad. Overall, ReCall had a higher success rate in user journey completions than Actor Who did.
