The Edinburgh City Council was concerned about the waste management and fly tipping problems in the city. Field research in selected neighborhoods, interviews, surveys, personas, journey maps, and a review of the existing mobile app revealead pain points in the existing service design. Based on the findings, a new design proposal was presented to the Edinburgh City Council.

Field Research

Leith Walk was a targetted neighborhood in the city for its severity in lack of waste management. Observations and talking to people in the area helped in gaining a better understanding of the underlying problems.

Observations + Interview Insights

+ People want to recycling but often, the bins that they have to walk to are already full

+ Flytipping of mattresses is common because of the hefty disposal pickup fees

+ We encountered no one that used the Edinburgh City Waste Management App

Mobile App Heuristic Evaluation

What Worked

+ Starting the user flow with the 'type of waste' selection

+ Attempting to add a bit of personality with the fun fact

+ Map view to show nearby bins

Improvements

+ Specify with the color scheme instead of type of recycling

+ Consistently use recycling color codes throughout the app for ease and speed of understanding

+ Add an option to request mattress or furniture disposal

Proposed Design

+ emphasize the color codes for the various waste categories in the app

+ adding alerts on how full the bins are will prevent wasted efforts in taking out the trash

+ added the option to contact the council for disposal of large furniture
